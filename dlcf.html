Project Overview

The Deeper Life Campus Fellowship (DLCF) FUPRE Academic Unit needed consistent, well-designed social media content to keep students engaged academically, particularly during exam seasons and semester pressure points. The audience: undergraduates who needed motivation without feeling lectured at, practical tools without feeling like homework.

I built a recurring content system with four distinct visual formats, each serving a different communication goal while remaining recognisably part of one brand. The system was designed to be repeatable — new content could be produced in each format without starting from scratch every time.

The Problem

Most campus Christian organisation content defaults to gold-and-white stock imagery with Bible verses in decorative fonts. That aesthetic is familiar to the point of invisibility. Students scroll past it without processing it. The visual language signals "this is content I already know" before anyone has read a word.

The secondary problem: generic motivational content has no specific relevance to the student's actual situation. "You can do it" works for nobody. "You have CSC317 in three days and you haven't opened the notes" is for the specific student who needs to act right now.

The brief: Create content that feels real, specific to campus life at FUPRE, and visually sharp enough to stop a scroll and earn a share — without losing the fellowship's identity.

Design Approach

Four content formats, each with a distinct visual language:

1. Motivation Campaign (Stay Grounded): Large-format typography, high-contrast palette, bold display type that reads at thumbnail size. Designed for Instagram shareability during exam pressure periods. The message does not pretend the pressure does not exist — it meets the student inside it.

2. Practical Checklist (Before-Class): Skeuomorphic notepad motif. Not inspiration — instruction. Students could screenshot and actually use it before their next lecture. Utility posts outperform motivational posts on retention because people come back to things they use.

3. Academic FAQ Series: Pill-label UI patterns borrowed from mobile apps. Conversational Q&A format answering real objections students use to justify skipping class. Non-preachy: it felt like a dialogue, not a sermon. The format made every card immediately shareable.

4. Quote Series: Frosted glass cards on study-desk photography backgrounds. Keyword highlights in the quote text. Designed to be saved and shared rather than just liked. The frosted glass treatment created depth without competing with the quote's message.

Color & Brand

Color was used as a signal, not decoration. A consistent accent color across the series makes every piece immediately recognisable as belonging to the same source. A student who sees one card in a friend's WhatsApp Status recognises the next one without needing to read the caption.

Typography: Lato Bold for headlines across the motivation and FAQ formats. Strong weight communicates authority without stiffness. Montserrat for body text and labels. The combination is familiar enough to read instantly, but the sizing and spacing decisions give it more personality than a default Canva template would produce.

The 5 Pieces

1. Stay Grounded Campaign

Mid-semester drift is a real and universal experience for FUPRE students — the point where early-semester discipline breaks down. This design acknowledged that reality directly rather than offering generic encouragement. Bold typographic layout, orange and midnight navy identity, built for Instagram shareability. Large display type that reads clearly at thumbnail size before the viewer decides to tap.

2. Before-Class Checklist

Part of the recurring #Dailyacademictips series. Tilted notepad motif that mimicked the visual logic of a real checklist — items spaced for readability, hierarchy clear — so it functioned as a genuine tool. Students who screenshotted this had something actionable before their next lecture, not just a feeling.

3. Academic FAQ Series

Converted recurring student questions into shareable designed cards. Conversational Q&A with yellow pill-label question formatting. Each card in the series was immediately recognisable as belonging to the same source. A student who shares one card is distributing the unit's brand without knowing it.

4 & 5. Quote Series (Vol. 1 & 2)

Frosted glass cards on study-desk photography backgrounds. Keyword highlights in the quote text create visual anchors for the key message. Designed to be saved and shared — the measurement for a quote card is saves, not likes. The frosted card treatment created visual depth without competing with the quote.
