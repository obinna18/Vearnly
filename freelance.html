Project Overview

Two independent freelance commissions with completely different briefs, audiences, and visual languages. AeroPure Mini targets health-conscious urban consumers in a product-launch context. Vina Beauté targets Lagos women with disposable income in a brand-launch context. The visual solutions are completely different — but the underlying design thinking is the same: what does the audience need to see in order to take the next step?

Both projects were delivered on freelance terms: brief, concept, iteration, final file. No templates. Built from the brief.

AeroPure Mini — The Brief

AeroPure Mini is a compact smart air purifier designed for urban households concerned about indoor air quality. The client needed a single-frame product launch ad for Instagram and print distribution. The ad needed to communicate what the product does, why it matters, and what to do next — in one visual, without overwhelming the viewer with technical specifications.

The constraint: A product ad for a health device is fighting against buyer skepticism. People have been sold air purifiers that didn't work. The design needed to earn trust before it earned a click.

The approach was to lead with outcome, not mechanism. Not "how the product works" but "what your life looks like with this product." Cleaner air. Healthier home. That is what earns attention before anyone is ready to read specs.

AeroPure Mini — Design Decisions

Layout structure: Three-column feature callouts arranged symmetrically around the hero product shot. The product occupies the center of the composition — this was non-negotiable. In product advertising, the product is the argument. Everything else supports it. The feature callouts are positioned to frame the product rather than compete with it.

Lung illustration overlay: A lung illustration superimposed on the product image creates immediate visual communication of the product's purpose before anyone reads the headline. Icon-level communication. The viewer understands "air + lungs + health" in under a second. That understanding is what makes them willing to read the supporting copy.

CTA design: Urgency framing with a deadline. "Available now — limited stock" creates scarcity without fabricating a countdown. The CTA is placed at the bottom of the composition where the eye naturally ends after moving through product, features, and supporting text. One clear next action: DM or click. Not a menu of options.

Teal and mint were chosen specifically because they are the colors the human brain associates with cleanliness, hospitals, and health. This is not a coincidence — it is the same reason medical devices, mouthwash brands, and cleaning products use this palette. The colors do the positioning before the viewer has consciously processed a single word. Cool tones signal air quality at a pre-cognitive level.

The white background amplifies the palette's effect by giving the cool tones maximum visibility and creating the sense of open, clean space that the product promises.

Vina Beauté — The Brief

Vina Beauté is a beauty studio based in Victoria Island, Lagos, offering premium services from bridal makeup to editorial glam. They were launching and needed a brand flyer that positioned them correctly: premium and aspirational, but warm and inviting enough to make someone want to book. Distribution: Instagram and WhatsApp.

The core tension: Luxury beauty brands in Lagos face a consistent challenge. Going too polished reads as cold and out of reach. Going too warm reads as budget. The goal was to hold both at once — premium enough to justify Victoria Island pricing, warm enough to make someone want to book.

The secondary challenge: Instagram and WhatsApp have different viewing contexts. On Instagram, the flyer competes in a grid view and needs to stand out before it is tapped. On WhatsApp, it is forwarded by existing customers and needs to communicate quality at small preview size. The design had to work in both contexts simultaneously.

Vina Beauté — Design Decisions

Photography as hero: The model photograph occupies the majority of the frame. In beauty marketing, the result is the product. Showing a glowing, confident woman communicates the outcome of booking before the viewer reads a single word. The photograph earns the viewer's attention. The copy converts it.

Frosted glass overlay: The service listing is presented on a frosted glass card overlaid on the photograph. This treatment creates visual depth and layering — the card floats in front of the image rather than blocking it. It is also practical: it makes the text readable on a busy photographic background without obscuring the model.

Typography: Bold serif display type for the brand name. Serifs in beauty branding carry associations with heritage, craft, and quality. The weight of the chosen serif communicates premium positioning at a glance. This is the visual shorthand for "we are not a budget salon" — not stated, not explained, just shown through font choice.

Composition flow: The eye enters at the model's face (top center), moves down to the brand name (center), across to the service listing (right panel), and ends at the contact information (bottom). The layout traces a natural reading path that ends at the booking information. You see the outcome, read the name, learn the services, then have everything you need to call.

Gold carries immediate cultural associations with premium and aspiration in the Nigerian beauty market. The choice was not decorative — it is positioning. Warm gold reads as luxury, not coldness. The rose undertone softened the gold so it communicated warmth alongside prestige. The palette was doing emotional work before a single word was read.

Deep black for the brand name typography creates maximum contrast against the gold and rose palette. Premium brands use black type, not coloured type, for their brand names. The contrast signals confidence.
