Project Overview

As Chapter Lead of the Data Scientists Network (DSN) at FUPRE, I was responsible for both running the chapter and designing the visuals that represented it. DSN is a national tech community operating through university chapters across Nigeria. At FUPRE, I took over the chapter in 2025 and needed to rebuild its visual presence to attract students who had never heard of data science, not just those already in the field.

The Harness the Holiday campaign was the primary design deliverable: a semester-break engagement campaign built to keep existing members active and drive new WhatsApp group joins through a QR code CTA.

The Problem

Tech communities on Nigerian campuses face a consistent engagement crisis during semester breaks. Students disengage, the WhatsApp group goes quiet, and when the semester resumes the community has to restart from near-zero energy. A chapter that goes silent in December reactivates to a colder audience in January.

The recruitment problem was separate but related: students who had never heard of DSN needed to understand what it was and why it was relevant to them in under five seconds of seeing a flyer. Most campus club recruitment graphics fail this test — they explain the club to people who already care, not to people who are indifferent.

The brief: Build a flyer that keeps existing members engaged during the semester break, drives new WhatsApp group joins through a QR code, and communicates "serious tech community" to a student who has never heard of DSN.

Design Approach

The QR code as a design element: The QR code was not an afterthought placed in a corner. It was sized and positioned bottom-center, large enough to scan from a phone screen without zooming. This is specifically for the way people interact with Instagram Reels and WhatsApp Status: watching from a distance, phone in hand. The code links directly to the DSN FUPRE WhatsApp group — no form, no link-in-bio, no barrier between impression and join.

"Harness the Holiday" framing: The headline reframes the semester break as an opportunity rather than a pause. This was a deliberate choice over a generic "join us" message. Students who see "Harness the Holiday" are being told: the break is not downtime, it is an advantage if you use it right. That framing creates FOMO and positions DSN as the vehicle for using the break productively.

Tech iconography: Circuit board patterns and data visualization motifs in the background communicate "this is a tech community" before the viewer reads a single word. A student who does not know what DSN is should be able to infer it from the visual language alone. The iconography does the positioning work that copy cannot do in under two seconds.

Color & Brand

Green and white against dark carries immediate associations with technology (WhatsApp, Nigeria's flag, developer tools). These are not arbitrary choices — they are the colors associated with credibility and tech in the Nigerian context. A student who sees them instinctively reads "professional" and "national" before processing a single word.

Typography

Headline: Bold condensed sans-serif for "Harness the Holiday." Condensed letterforms pack maximum visual weight into limited horizontal space. The headline needed to read clearly at thumbnail size — the decision to go condensed allowed it to be set at a size that commands the composition without crowding the QR code and supporting text.

Hierarchy: Strict three-level hierarchy across the flyer: chapter identity (DSN FUPRE) at the top, primary message (Harness the Holiday) in the dominant position, action instruction (scan to join) at the base with the QR code. Each level at a clearly distinct size. Nothing in the middle zone competed with the headline. The viewer's eye moves top to bottom without ambiguity about what to read next.

Body text: Lighter weight of the same condensed family for subheading and supporting text. One typeface family throughout. When a design is going to WhatsApp and Instagram where it will be viewed at small sizes on varied screens, typographic simplicity is a feature not a limitation.
